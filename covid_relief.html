Migrant Travel

June 27, 2018 at 2:22pm

We helped over 6000 migrants reach home during this period through buses, trains and flights. We facilitated travel to parts of Bihar, Jharkhand and Uttar Pradesh. We ensured that migrants received cooked meals, sanitary pads, packaged food, and other vital support throughout their journey.


We worked extensively with governments and other organizations to arrange trains for workers. We also arranged and sponsored travel tickets for migrants to return to their place of work.


Direct Benefit transfers

June 27, 2018 at 2:22pm

We arranged over 12000 cooked meals for migrants travelling with us with the help of partner organizations like Janman, the 100 Rupee Project, and through tie-ups with dhabas along bus routes.


We distributed over 400 ration kits. Each kit cost up to Rs 700 and lasted up to 15 days.


Food

June 27, 2018 at 2:22pm

We supported migrants who had lost their jobs or were in need of immediate financial aid with direct cash transfers (up to Rs 1500 per account). We also supported migrants with medical aid up to Rs 5000. We were able to support nearly 200 people through direct cash transfers.


Livelihood Restoration

June 27, 2018 at 2:22pm

We sponsored small businesses through the purchase of small equipment like sewing machines, electrical tools and other raw material. We also supported migrants with payment of rent. We provided support up to Rs 4000. We worked extensively with migrants, identified their skill sets, needs and requirements and helped them connect to employers.


Financial Aid Towards Education

June 27, 2018 at 2:22pm

We supported students struggling with online education through purchase, procurement and distribution of new and second-hand smartphones and other gadgets. This was possible through the support of partner organizations like Aaghaz Foundation.


Support For Front Line workers

June 27, 2018 at 2:22pm

Smart Phone Distribution for Online Education

June 27, 2018 at 2:22pm

Yasmeen is preparing for her admission in JMI, but her phone crashed between her classes so she couldn’t attend online tuitions. Similarly, 8 year old Tashif wanted admission in 3rd grade, but his family had no source of income or savings left for his admission fees. They were planning to drop his schooling for a year. We got in touch with Aaghaz Foundation and they arranged for a smart phone for Yasmeen, and Tashif’s fees in no time! Similarly we have arranged 7 smartphone for the children of Migrants worker which helped them in continuing Online education.
